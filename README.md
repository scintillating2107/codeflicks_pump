    <h2>Experiment</h2>

    <p><strong>Objective:</strong> To study the working principle of a centrifugal pump and determine its efficiency experimentally.</p>

    <p class="section-title">Apparatus Required:</p>
    <ul>
      <li>Centrifugal pump test rig</li>
      <li>Pressure gauges</li>
      <li>Flow measuring device (e.g., rotameter)</li>
      <li>Energy meter</li>
      <li>Stopwatch</li>
      <li>Measuring tank</li>
    </ul>

    <p class="section-title">Theory:</p>
    <p>
      A centrifugal pump converts mechanical energy into hydraulic energy by means of centrifugal force. The pump consists of an impeller which rotates inside a casing. As the impeller rotates, it imparts kinetic energy to the fluid. The kinetic energy is then converted into pressure energy in the casing.
    </p>

    <p class="section-title">Procedure:</p>
    <ol>
      <li>Prime the pump to remove any air inside the casing.</li>
      <li>Start the pump and allow it to run at the rated speed.</li>
      <li>Note the discharge using the flow meter.</li>
      <li>Measure the suction and delivery head using pressure gauges.</li>
      <li>Record the input power using an energy meter or wattmeter.</li>
      <li>Repeat the experiment for various flow rates by adjusting the delivery valve.</li>
    </ol>

    <p class="section-title">Observations:</p>
    <ul>
      <li>Suction head (Hs)</li>
      <li>Delivery head (Hd)</li>
      <li>Total head (H = Hs + Hd)</li>
      <li>Discharge (Q)</li>
      <li>Input power (Pin)</li>
      <li>Output power (Pout = ρgQH)</li>
    </ul>

    <p class="section-title">Calculations:</p>
    <p>
      Efficiency (η) = (Output Power / Input Power) × 100<br>
      Output Power = ρ × g × Q × H (in watts)
    </p>

    <p class="section-title">Result:</p>
    <p>
      The efficiency of the centrifugal pump was found to be ____ %.
    </p>

    <p class="section-title">Conclusion:</p>
    <p>
      The working of a centrifugal pump was studied and its efficiency was calculated based on input and output power.
    </p>
